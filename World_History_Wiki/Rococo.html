In the early 18th century Rococo was an art movement that originated in Paris, France. The word ‘rococo’ derives from rocaille, which is french for rubble or rock. Some often referred this style of artwork as late Baroque. In the 1730’s to 1770’s it was a very fashionable way of composing art. The Rococo style started with the decorative arts of interior design which includes , paintings, architecture and sculpture, and later grew to include theater design and music. There were many different words to describe the Rococo style but a few common words that were used for this style of artwork was whimsical and mischievous. It is also considered the most influential of the french art movements. The style then spread quickly through the rest of France and then to Germany, Austria, England, and other European Countries. Jean- Antoine Watteau is credited with the birth of rococo paintings. Jean invented a new genre called fetes galantes, which were scenes of courtship parties. Jean was influenced by genre scenes of everyday life that were quite popular in flanders and the netherlands. He is known for his paintings of elegantly dressed figures gathered in outdoor spaces, exchanging pleasantries and enjoying music. Also a Famous rococo artist would be Francois Boucher, he referred to the pastel pinks and blues. Rococo is a cheery style of art that involves the heavy use of frilly ornamentation, along with sweeping curves, and asymmetry. It showcases an exquisite attention to detail. Rococo paintings are also characterized by soft pastel colors, curvy lines, depicts scenes of amorous encounters, light hearted entertainment, levity, and floral motifs and also used muted colors, asymmetrical lines to combine with themes of sensual love and nature. Rococo artists goal was to create a flow in their paintings. These artists did not like the boxy type shapes instead they liked the sweep curves and asymmetry and used different types of colors than the late Baroque. Rococo Artists used a variety of shades like pink, blue, occasional bursts of orange, yellow, and green. Where as, Baroque paintings are associated with deep colors and emotive shadows and Rococo paintings are airy pastel. Rococo Art and Architecture was highly decorative walls and ceilings in a oval shape chamber. Rococo Sculpture makes use of very delicate porcelain instead of marble or another heavy material. Rococo artists goal was to create a flow in their paintings. Rococo music was superficial, light, elegant, and was also known as very classical.
