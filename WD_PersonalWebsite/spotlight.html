Highlighted Event

Adopting Fozzie

We adopted Fozzie just a few days after he was old enough to be adopted. He was small enough to where you could

fit him into one hand. When we first brought him home, he was shy at first, which is to be expected. After about a day

or two staying in my room, however, he began to warm up to me, and then everyone else slowly. He became very

snuggly, even doing that rolling thing that Holly does whenever she's happy. The picture to the left shows him doing

this while on top of his cat climber. It wasn't just limited to his climber either; anything he could find, he would snuggle

with. This included: My gaming chair - especially whenever I was sitting in it (seen below, to the right), a stuffed tiger

plush that I keep on my floor, my couch, and even inside my dresser. After it was okay to

finally let him out and explore the rest of the house, the fun still never stopped for him.

By this, I mean he was a flat out goof ball in many more ways than one. One major

example of this (once again, seen below to the left), is him deciding that he would fight

off anyone who tried to steal the laundry, which in itself is too cute to pass up a picture.

From there on, it's been a lot of fun whenever Fozzie is around, which can be taken in

one of two ways. For one, watching him act like a total goofball is oddly entertaining

enough to watch in itself; but on the other hand, when you have to literally pull him away

from other cats so they don't end up fighting to the death constantly, it can get a little

old.
